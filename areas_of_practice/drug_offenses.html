In the Alberta drug possession criminal code, there exists a question of whether or not the evidence against you was obtained lawfully. Drug arrest evidence can’t be used against you, if the police performed an illegal search and seizure to obtain the evidence. In preparation for a trial, our lawyers will endeavour to counter the case put forward by the prosecution. To carry this out we look at key areas:

  • Is the evidence put forward against you objectionable or faulty?
  • In the case of Search Warrant, was a proper Order issued?
  • Did proper Search Warrant procedures occur?
  • Did Reasonable and Probable Grounds exist to issue the search warrant?
  • In the case of a wiretap, were reasonable and probable grounds met?
